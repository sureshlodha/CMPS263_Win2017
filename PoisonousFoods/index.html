How much contaminants could we consume everyday?

Because of air, water, and soil pollution, small traces of contaminants like lead, cadmium, and arsenic can be found in foods that we consume on a daily basis. Contaminants like cadmium can accumulate in our bodies for many years, and can have adverse health and cognitive effects. This is especially concerning for infants and children in poor communities who are more likely to be exposed to higher amount of contaminants. 10 mcg/dL (microgram/deciliter) is enough to harm a child's intelligence[1][2].
